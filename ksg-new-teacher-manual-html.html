1. Introduction to KSG

KSG (Kids Small Group) is a program designed to teach children about God, Jesus, and Christian principles. As a teacher, your role is crucial in shaping the spiritual growth of these young minds.

Your Role as a KSG Teacher

  1. Be a positive role model
  2. Create a welcoming and safe environment
  3. Teach biblical principles in an engaging way
  4. Foster a love for learning about God
  5. Collaborate with co-leaders and parents

Remember, you're not just teaching lessons; you're helping shape young lives and their relationship with God.

2. Classroom Setup and Management

Room Setup: Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Arrive early (at least 15-20 minutes before class starts)
  2. Arrange seating in a way that promotes interaction (e.g., circle or small groups)
  3. Set up activity stations if applicable
  4. Prepare materials for the day's lesson
  5. Create a visible schedule of activities (more on this below)
  6. Set up a prayer request area
  7. Ensure the room is clean and welcoming

Creating and Using a Visual Schedule

  1. Use a whiteboard or large poster board
  2. List activities in order (e.g., Welcome, Opening Prayer, Lesson, Activity, Snack, Closing)
  3. Include approximate times for each activity
  4. Use pictures or symbols for younger children
  5. Review the schedule at the beginning of class
  6. Reference the schedule during transitions

Managing Transitions: A Process

  1. Give a 5-minute warning before the end of an activity
  2. Use a countdown technique (e.g., "We're switching activities in 5...4...3...2...1")
  3. Use a consistent signal (e.g., clapping pattern, special sound)
  4. Clearly state the next activity
  5. Have materials for the next activity ready
  6. Praise students who transition quickly and quietly

Story: The Importance of Schedules

Natasha shared a story about a child who was sitting just outside the circle. The leaders viewed it as non-compliance, but it was likely a coping mechanism for the child's anxiety. Having a visible schedule can help such children feel more secure, knowing what's coming next.

3. Engaging Students

Interactive Learning Techniques

  1. Total Physical Response (TPR)
    • Incorporate movements into lessons
    • Example: Teaching the SHAPE acronym with gestures
      • S (Spiritual Gifts): Raise hands
      • H (Heart): Place hands over heart
      • A (Abilities): Flex arms
      • P (Personality): Point to smile
      • E (Experiences): Gesture broadly
  2. Active Learning Strategies
    • Use role-play for Bible stories
    • Incorporate games that reinforce lesson themes
    • Create art projects related to the lesson
    • Use small group discussions for older children

Keeping Students Engaged: Step-by-Step

  1. Start with an engaging activity as students arrive
    • Set up coloring sheets or word searches related to the lesson
    • Have a simple puzzle or game available
  2. Begin the lesson with an attention-grabber
    • Ask a thought-provoking question
    • Share a brief, relevant story
    • Show an interesting object related to the lesson
  3. Break up the lesson into shorter segments
  4. Incorporate movement between segments
  5. Use varied teaching methods (visual, auditory, kinesthetic)
  6. Encourage student participation through questions and activities
  7. End with a memorable activity or challenge related to the lesson

Story: Making Lessons Stick

Cassie shared how she taught the SHAPE concept using motions. Even years later, students remembered the gestures and the meaning behind them. This shows the power of incorporating movement and interactive elements into your lessons.

9. Positive Reinforcement and Rewards

Implementing a Positive Reinforcement System

  1. Clearly communicate expectations
  2. Catch students being good and praise specifically
  3. Use a visible tracking system (e.g., sticker chart, token economy)
  4. Offer individual and group rewards
  5. Celebrate small victories and improvements

Ideas for Classroom Responsibilities

  1. Line Leader: Leads the class when moving to different areas
  2. Prayer Warrior: Collects and reads prayer requests
  3. Attendance Helper: Assists with checking in students
  4. Scripture Reader: Reads the Bible verse for the lesson
  5. Clean-up Captain: Oversees classroom tidying
  6. Snack Distributor: Helps hand out snacks
  7. Greeter: Welcomes new students to the class

Non-Monetary Reward Ideas

  1. Extra playground or game time
  2. Pajama day
  3. Bring a stuffed animal to class day
  4. Student's choice of worship song
  5. Special seat or cushion for the day
  6. Be the teacher's helper
  7. Choose the next class game

Implementing Class-Wide Goals

  1. Set a achievable goal (e.g., everyone learns the memory verse)
  2. Choose a reward (e.g., ice cream party, movie day)
  3. Track progress visually
  4. Encourage peer support and teamwork
  5. Celebrate when the goal is reached

Remember, your role as a KSG teacher is vital. Stay positive, be prepared, and don't hesitate to ask for help from your coaches and co-leaders. Your dedication and these strategies will help create a vibrant, engaging KSG experience that impacts children's lives and spiritual growth.
